Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords or bank details.

In a typical phishing attack, the attacker sends a fake email or message pretending to be from a trusted organization like a bank or online service. The message often contains a link that leads to a fake website. When the user enters their login details, the attacker captures this information.

Students and online users are common targets of phishing attacks. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

This type of cybercrime is increasing due to the growing use of digital platforms.
